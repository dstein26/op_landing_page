Many Styles to try

There are many different styles of beer each with their own color, feel, and flavor profile.

Here I will explore some of the different styles you can find.

Ingredients

Below are the primary ingredients that go into making beer

Grain

Often using malted barley or wheat to add sugars for fermentation to the brew as well as color, flavor, and aroma

Yeast

Yeast is a single-celled micro-organism responsible for the fermentation process in beer making. This process converts sugars into alcohol and carbon dioxide.

Hops

Hops are a plant that is used in flavoring beer. Hops are responsible for the strong bitter flavor in some beers.

Water

Water makes up the majority of the beer's ingredients.

These four simple ingredients are all that is required to make beer.

Styles

The main two categories of beer are ales and lagers.

Ales

Ales are made with "top-fermenting" yeasts at relativley warm tempertures

Lagers

Lagers are made with "bottom-fermenting" yeasts at lower temperatures. They are then usually lagered, or be stored and conditioned for a while after fermentation.

Stout

Rich tasting of toasted malt and hints of chocolate or coffee. Feels thick and creamy.

American Pale Ale

Floral and citrusy hop flavors are the dominate flavors with a medium body.

Hefeweizen

A light-colored cloudy beer with low bitterness and some banana fruity flavors

Pilsner

Light colored clear beer with usually a balanced hop bitterness with German noble hops. One of the most common beer styles

Vienna Lager

Usually darker than the Pilsner and a stronger malty flavor/aroma

Maerzen

Clean, balanced beer with a toasted bread or biscuit flavor and aroma profile. The style most commonly associated with Octoberfest.
